Strategies and Experiments on Fair and Transparent Recommendations

Aleksandr Popov

09.03.23

Introduction

The goal of Fairness-aware Recommender Systems is to optimize the fairness constraints, rather than only optimizing for accuracy.

  • On user's side: demographic/socio-economic data biases, increase trust on platform
  • On companies' side: suboptimal performance (e.g. activity bias), regulator obligations

The design, implementation, and deployment of such systems require the active involvement of practitioners, regulators, and owners who hold essential responsibilities.

Research Design

  • Identifying unfairness in the algorithm:

    • System latency bias for items and discrimination of disadvantage groups for users.


  • Derive metrics for fairness evaluation

    • Equality of opportunity, demographic parity, user-oriented group fairness, etc.

  • Integrating fairness constraints into the algorithm

    • e.g. tuning ranking algorithm to optimize both relevance and fairness.


  • Results validation
    • offline: direct evaluation or simulation based on distribution of protected attributes;
    • online: A/B testing to make sure that buisness metics (CTR, VTR, conversion rates) are satisfied.

Presentation Strategies

Presentation in the form of banners, pre- and mid-rolls or pop-ups can be an element of the experimental loop, as advertising attributes can be set as additional optimisation parameters (e.g. intelligent partial pooling) within existing framework.
