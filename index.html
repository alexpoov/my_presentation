But one though got me thinking.. I mean, really hard..

What enables these research groups to consistently generate (1) so many (2) impactful studies?
What constraints do they not experience?

Factors that I found (buy "asking" research method):
  • more people -> more research
    (2-4.5 phd/professors ratios)
  • grants, grants, grants
  • people have protected time to do research
    • teaching takes a lot of time
    • experimental research design is time-consuming
How long does it take to conduct an (experimental) study? The case of some random Sasha Popov:
  • May'24 - idea formulated
  • September'24 - first online survey experiment
  • November'24 - first mock exam experiment
  • April'25 - second mock exam experiment
    • -- You are somewhere here! --
  • Autumn'25 (hopefully) - second online survey experiment
  • January'26 (hopefully) - first paper submission
What could be done collectively to make research cycles more efficient?

From LA / EDM perspectives, I think we can...

  • Engage students/GLs in hypotheses formulation
  • Diversify data collection process
The rich world of educational activities - INF100 case (excluding learning design):

  • Lectures - we record them
  • Group meetings - ?
  • Labs - grades (very coarse-grained)
  • Exams - grades, timestamps
We have a great overview on student's performance (both prior and exam), which is great (especially considering high-stake examination nature of our courses).

However, we are missing any evidence
on learning process.

What analytics can add to didactics-focused research?
  • new data can drive instructional theory development.
  • facilitate interactions between research, practitioners, and administration.
  • promote interdisciplinary research.
  • assist in conducting "in vivo" experiments with less concerns in generalization.
And 2 very best things here:

  • We already have a field that is the easiest to collect learning evidence from!
  • We have a SLATE group specializing on learning analytics!
What can we start from?

  • Test LAIS design on new course (lower risks and costs of changes).
  • Thinking in terms "why" and "what" data can we collect:
    • what kinds of improvement [in education] we seek to make,
    • the most productive paths towards them, and
    • how to generate compelling evidence of the positive changes possible
  • Review code analysis tools (e.g. building knowledge component model of unparsable code)
