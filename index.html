Strategies and Experiments on Fair and Transparent Recommendations

Aleksandr Popov

09.03.23

Introduction

The goal of Fairness-aware Recommender Systems is to optimize the fairness constraints, rather than only optimizing for accuracy.

  • preprocessing techniques are designed to modify the input data
  • algorithm modification techniques to create fair modeling under any inputs
  • postprocessing techniques to modify models outputs

The design, implementation, and deployment of such systems require the active involvement of practitioners, regulators, and owners who hold essential responsibilities.

The motivation behind Fairness-aware algorithms:

  • On user's side: demographic/socio-economic data biases, increase trust on platform
  • On companies' side: suboptimal performance (e.g. activity bias), regulator obligations, social responsibilitiy.

Research Design

A case of ads recommendations in VoD services


  • Identifying unfairness in the algorithm:

    • System latency bias for items and discrimination of disadvantage groups for users. This could include: disproportional shows, targeting on protected attributes, user's activity bias.


  • Derive metrics for fairness evaluation

    • Deriving fairness metrics - this step has several well-performed practices (i.e. demographic parity, equity of opportunity), but it should be separately formulated for specific platform's needs.

  • Integrating fairness constraints into the algorithm

    • Constraint-based optimization: regularization over model predictions, adversarial learning.


  • Results validation
    • offline: direct evaluation on data or simulation based on distribution of protected attributes;
    • online: A/B testing to make sure that buisness metics (CTR, VTR, conversion rates) are satisfied.

Presentation Strategies

Presentation in the form of banners, pre- and mid-rolls or pop-ups can be an element of the experimental loop, as advertising attributes can be set as additional optimisation parameters (e.g. intelligent partial pooling) within existing framework.

Parallels with current research

Implementation of Self-Deployed Behavioural Interventions in Educational Decision-Making under Uncertainty


In this work the challenges of decision-making in the field of education are discussed. It is assumed that Integrating self-deployed behavioural interventions into the design of educational systems can improve awareness and transparency of decision-making experience. We plan to construct a framework with user experiment to model (statically and dynamically) user's behaviour and test the effectiveness of different behavioural interventions in designing learning systems.

  • Decision-making problem optimization for two-sided digital platform: students and academic programs/departments
  • Preference elicitation based on user's interactions and item's metadata
  • Dynamic modelling of behaviour for consequent Bayesian optimisation
